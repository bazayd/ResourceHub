Student Success : A Resource Hub

A resource hub for students by Students
Students often have a hard time finding resources for aid, transportation, and textbooks;
often making them feel stressed about financials, and many students already have enough stuff on their plate. We're here to help.

Cars and Public Transportation

As a college student, it is often hard to find good car that won't leave you stranded if you don't have much money or any credit to finance anything newer.
In the bay area, some colleges offer free clipper cards for students to use on the BART train and VTA buses.

Financial Aid and Scholarships

You can visit FAFSA's website to learn more about the application process and if you are eligible.
A few scholarship websites where you can find good opportunities are College Online and Grants where many are centralized around some ethnic groups!

Textbooks

Some professors like to go overboard with their textbook prices, I remember I had to buy a $120 textbook. I didn't even read the entire textbook!
So here at Student Resources we'll provide some websites that give textbooks at a cheaper price and some even for free.
Textbooks for Life has a good selection of cheap textbooks for a variety of classes.
Project Gutenberg provides a lot of good free eBooks for many classes as well!

Finances

It is often hard to manage your money as a college student, sometimes it is hard to really find a plan.
If you never had the best budgeting skills or if you've budgeted before and want to learn new budgeting skills we are here to help!
The 50/30/20 method is a great way to pay off expenses and then save some money on the side while also having extra money to have fun!
